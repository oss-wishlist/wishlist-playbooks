# Digital Sovereignty Playbook (Canada — Education)

Playbook for educational institutions and practitioners to evaluate technology services, reduce dependency risk, and improve digital sovereignty through sustainable open source and vendor-neutral infrastructure, with consideration for Canadian education procurement, privacy, and data residency requirements.

This work focuses on:
- Canadian data residency for student, research, and institutional data  
- reducing reliance on single vendors or jurisdictions  
- strengthening sustainability of critical open source dependencies  
- improving operational continuity and institutional control  
- supporting education procurement and compliance requirements  
- enabling long-term maintainability and portability  

---

## Process Milestones

Note: Some milestones may not apply depending on procurement stage or service maturity. Documentation for future consideration is encouraged.

1. Kickoff: Institution, OSS Wishlist admin, and practitioner align on sovereignty goals and evaluation scope  
2. Service & Dependency Mapping  
3. Student Data & Residency Risk Assessment  
4. Vendor Lock-In & Jurisdiction Risk Review  
5. Open Source Dependency Sustainability Review  
6. Education Procurement & Compliance Assessment  
7. Sovereignty Improvement Plan & Implementation  
8. Wrap up meeting: Institution and practitioner review outcomes  
9. Survey (institution and practitioner)

# Digital Sovereignty & Dependency Sustainability – Education Peer Review Rubric

**Purpose:** Evaluate how well a technology service used in education can be adopted, operated, and sustained while maintaining Canadian data residency, reducing vendor lock-in, and ensuring long-term institutional control.

**Scoring Scale per Criterion:**  
0 = Not present  
1 = Minimal / high risk  
2 = Adequate but fragile  
3 = Strong and sustainable  
4 = Sovereign and education-ready

---

## A. Service & Dependency Visibility (0–12 pts)

| Criterion | Indicators of Excellence | Score |
|---------|--------------------------|------|
| **A1. Service Dependency Inventory** | Core services, SaaS components, and integrations documented. | 0–4 |
| **A2. Critical Education Function Identification** | Services handling student, LMS, research, or identity data identified. | 0–4 |
| **A3. Ownership & Control Awareness** | Vendors, operators, and maintainers clearly understood. | 0–4 |
